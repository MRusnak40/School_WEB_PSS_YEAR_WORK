Be Healthier 💪

Discover tips and tricks for a healthier lifestyle

Nutritons

The human body requires about 16 essential minerals 🪨 to function properly and stay healthy.

You can get these vital nutrients by eating a balanced diet rich in leafy greens 🥬, nuts 🌰, seeds, and whole grains 🌾.

However, athletes 🏃 often lose crucial minerals through sweat 💦 and intense physical exertion.

The most common mineral deficiencies in active individuals include:

  • Magnesium
  • Iron
  • Zinc
  • Calcium 🥛
  • K2
  • D3

Replenishing specific minerals is key to maintaining peak energy ⚡ and supporting fast muscle recovery 💪.

What is bad food?

Modern diets are often filled with ultra-processed foods 🍕 that provide calories but lack essential nutrients.

These products are typically engineered with excessive sodium 🧂, which can lead to high blood pressure and dehydration.

Many of these foods contain refined seed oils 🌻 and trans fats that may trigger inflammation throughout the body.

The main characteristics of "bad fuel" for your body include:

  • Added Sugars 🍬
  • Artificial Preservatives 🧪
  • Low-Quality Fats 🍟
  • High Glycemic Index 📈

Choosing whole, real foods over processed alternatives is the simplest way to boost your long-term health 🍎.

What should I eat?

Your body needs many essential minerals 🪨 to stay healthy and keep your energy stable.

You get them by eating a varied diet with leafy greens 🥬, nuts 🌰, seeds, fruits, and whole grains 🌾.

Leafy greens are especially useful because they provide magnesium, calcium, iron and antioxidants that support muscles, recovery, and overall health.

Why a varied diet matters:

  • Different foods give you different nutrients — no single food has everything.
  • Colorful vegetables add antioxidants that protect your cells.
  • Whole grains and legumes support long-lasting energy.
  • Nuts and seeds add healthy fats and important minerals.

Why we should rotate foods:

  • Prevents nutrient deficiencies.
  • Supports digestion with different types of fiber.
  • Helps keep your diet balanced and not overloaded with one food.
  • Makes healthy eating easier to maintain.

Why is sleep important?

Quality sleep is essential for your energy, focus, recovery, and overall health. During sleep, your body repairs tissues, restores the brain, and balances important hormones.

Good sleep helps you stay mentally sharp, improves memory, and supports stable mood throughout the day.

What should you know :

  • Sleep deprivation
  • States of sleep
  • Sleep Lenght calc

Why consistent sleep matters:

  • Going to bed at similar times keeps your internal clock stable.
  • Consistent sleep improves daily energy and concentration.
  • It reduces stress and supports emotional balance.
  • It helps your body maintain healthy recovery and performance.

How does a good lifestyle look?

A healthy lifestyle combines movement, sunlight, fresh air and balanced daily habits that support your body and mind.

Regular physical activity helps your muscles, posture, and energy levels, especially if you spend most of the day sitting.

Sunlight and time outside support your mood, vitamin D levels, and natural daily rhythm.

Key elements of a good lifestyle:

  • Daily movement — walking, stretching, or light exercise keeps your body active and reduces stiffness.
  • Sunlight exposure — spending time outside helps regulate sleep and boosts overall well‑being.
  • Fresh air — being outdoors improves focus, reduces stress, and supports healthy breathing.
  • Balancing sitting time — long sitting should be broken up with short movement breaks.

How much movement do you need if you sit all day?

  • Short breaks every 45–60 minutes help reduce tension and improve circulation.
  • At least 30–60 minutes of daily activity supports energy and long‑term health.
  • More intense exercise can help balance a fully sedentary day.
